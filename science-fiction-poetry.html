Dianoia Collection

The Dianoia Collection is comprised of haikus on various science-fictional themes (as well as about the genre), including references to particular authors, stories, or worldbuilding contexts. In a strict sense, however, especially in the thematic aspect, they are not traditional Japanese haiku, nor are they precisely senryū, either. Nonetheless, I like adhering to the typical structure, concerning (1) metrics, for which I use syllables and not morae or onji, in part because I do not write them in Japanese (perhaps some day), as well as, and more significantly, (2) by seeking to pivot, inspired by the disjuncture that is a distinguishing feature of haiku, and which is mediated in Japanese by the 切れ字 (kireji, ‘cutting word’). Very importantly, in the spirit of this kind of poetry, I enjoy practising its density in brevity, while playing with intuition, in connection with the meditative attitude it tends to have. For pragmatism, therefore, whenever I use the term “haiku” I mean it sensu lato, mostly in reference to my own writings. For greater accuracy, I can suggest expressions like “haikuesque” or “haiku-like” instead, as I am sure others have, but “haiku” is better-known across the globe, and its common usage in the Occidental world started in the XIX century.

I

Ia

Speculative hill
where I study strange flowers
aroma of stars.

Ib

In synthetic tales
I swervingly analyse
analog planets.

Ic

By means of fiction
re-approach reality
distance affection.

II

IIa

Stellar radiance
which we sense from our capsules
we focus mirrors.

IIb

Archaeologists
interpret ancient stargates
future threshold views.

IIc

Beautiful cluster
but with blood-tinged nebulae
the free roamers fly.

IId

Simulacrum field,
anarcho-capitalist?
Infomorphs collate.

IIe

Cosmic bonfires
background of our travels
nomadic suns flow.

IIf

Orbital stations
rootless cosmopolites rest
vessels awaken.

IIg

Transneural scanners
in hydrostatic capsules
clone bodies await.

III

IIIa

Searching urbanite
experiences wanderthought
mystified frontiers.

IIIb

Post-scarcity son
beneath a conditioned dome
horizon instinct.

IV

IVa

The galactic state
pictures a classical past
potential present.

IVb

Mathematicians
err psychohistorically
non-linear ways.

V

We alternate views
to see juxtaposed cultures
world sign variance.

VI

VIa

They nourish neurons
with subcellular machines
cognitive mists swirl.

VIb

They sustain axons
linking throngs of iotas
kinesis endures.

VII

Cyborg nutrition
from molecule assemblers
a spoon feeds itself.

VIII

Scientists approach
mercury cnidarians
in Yatsushiro.
