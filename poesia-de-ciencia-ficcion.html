Dianoia Collection

The Dianoia Collection is comprised of haikus on various science-fictional themes (as well as about the genre), including references to particular authors, stories, or worldbuilding contexts. In a strict sense, however, especially in the thematic aspect, they are not traditional Japanese haiku, nor are they precisely senryū, either. Nonetheless, I like adhering to a typical structure. In detail, I consider the following:

  • Metrics, constructing with the Spanish syllables, the quantity of which occasionally varies by one or two. I do not use onji given that I do not write in Japanese (perhaps some day). Initially, I was writing haiku in English with the intention of reaching more readers around the globe. There persisted the idea of taking advantage of being a native speaker of a Romance language and its syllabic prosody, so I decided to transform all my haiku and rewrite them in Spanish. What is currently shown is first the Spanish original and then its English translation, whose metrics may differ, therefore. In that aspect of anglophone haiku I now think of morae instead of syllables. This is mainly considering long vowels, as well as, in the decreasing diphthongs of English (with Scottish pronunciations as exceptions), the tendency to the second vocoid not being a semivowel, unlike in Spanish (and other Romance languages). Sometimes my English translations fully adhere to the pattern, either in syllables or in morae. This happens accidentally, which I enjoy. In any case, I do not think of haiku or other poetry as reducible to metrics.
  • More significantly, I seek to pivot, inspired by the disjuncture that is a distinguishing feature of haiku and which traditionally is mediated in Japanese by the 切れ字 (kireji) ‘cutting word’.
  • Concomitantly with the previous item, while authentic haiku regularly juxtaposes two images or other sensory instances, my derivatives not necessarily, and many times they do not do so. What they frequently juxtapose are concepts, or thought processes instead of qualia processes, especially the philosophical haiku.
  • Understandably, my derivatives do not allude to the four seasons either, so they do not necessarily have an analogue of the 季語 (kigo) ‘seasonal word’. However, relevant to the science fiction haiku, it is reasonable for them to contain certain keywords pertaining to the iconography of the genre (a bit more about this in section “On Science Fiction”).
  • Essential in the spirit of this kind of poetry, I enjoy practising its density in brevity, while playing with intuition —which leaps over the hiatus effectuated by the cut—, in connection with the meditative attitude it cultivates.

For pragmatism, then, when I use the term “haiku” I do so sensu lato, mostly in reference to my own writings. For greater accuracy, I can suggest expressions like “haikuesque” or “haiku-like”, as surely others have, but “haiku” is better-known as generic name, having entered the vocabularies of the Occidental world at the end of the XIX century, to later spread in the middle of the XX. Furthermore, for those of us who cannot at present read haiku in Japanese, here is something superlatively valuable: the transcriptions of literal translations, element by element, “incomplete”, in contrast to relying only on final translations, fully developed, “complete” (the latter, of course, have their interpretative value, and at the same time let us remember the Italian adage traduttore, traditore ‘translator, traitor’). It has been constructive when learning from the oeuvre of Matsuo Bashō, Yosa Buson, Kobayashi Issa and Masaoka Shiki. For that I can recommend An Introduction to Haiku: An Anthology of Poems and Poets from Bashō to Shiki (1958), by USA author Harold G. Henderson.

I

Ia

Llanura extraña,
flores luminiscentes;
aroma estelar.

Strange plains,
luminiscent flowers;
stellar aroma.

Ib

Montaña extraña,
árboles trashumantes;
frutos astrales.

Strange mountain,
transhumant trees;
astral fruits.

II

IIa

Cuentos de otredad:
planetas análogos,
órbitas nuevas.

Tales of otherness:
analog planets,
new orbits.

IIb

Ficción mediata,
reaproximación real;
circuitos fluyen.

Mediate fiction,
real reapproximation;
circuits flow.

III

IIIa

Orbes brillantes,
cápsulas sensoriales;
ajusto espejos.

Brilliant orbs,
sensory capsules;
I adjust mirrors.

IIIb

Escáner neural,
cápsula hidrostática;
cuerpos clónicos.

Transneural scanner,
hydrostatic capsules;
clonic bodies.

IV

Arqueólogos:
puerta estelar antigua,
umbral futuro.

Archaeologists:
ancient stargate,
future threshold.

V

Bello cúmulo,
mas sangre en nebulosas;
vuelo en libertad.

Beautiful cluster,
but blood on nebulae;
I fly freely.

VI

Cual simulacro
anarcocapitalista;
psique infomorfa.

Simulacrum-like,
anarcho-capitalist;
infomorph psyche.

VII

VIIa

Hogar de helio:
trasfondo peregrino,
soles nómadas.

Helium hearth:
pilgrim background,
nomadic suns.

VIIb

Albergue orbital:
cosmopolita sin raíz
reposa y se va.

Orbital lodgings:
rootless cosmopolite
reposes and leaves.

VIII

VIIIa

Mozo urbanita,
pensamiento buscador;
liminaridad.

Urbanite lad,
searching thought;
liminality.

VIIIb

Tal postescasez:
domo acondicionado,
bordes místicos.

Such post-scarcity:
conditioned dome,
mystic borders.

VIIIc

Urbe en páramo,
plexo computacional:
memoria antigua.

City in wasteland,
computational plexus:
ancient memory.

IX

IXa

Galaxia-estado:
de pasados clásicos
a hoy potencial.

Galaxy-state:
from classical pasts
to potential today.

IXb

Cálculo enorme,
error psicohistórico;
flujo no lineal.

Enormous calculation,
psychohistorical error;
non-linear flux.

X

Vistas alternas,
culturas yuxtapuestas;
signos de mundo.

We alternate views
to see juxtaposed cultures
world sign variance.

XI

XIa

Impulso neural,
máquina subcelular;
niebla cuántica.

Neural impulse,
subcellular machine;
quantum mist.

XIb

Vigor de axones
en potenciales de acción:
neokinesia.

Axons' vigour
in action potentials:
neokinesis.

XII

Nutrición cíborg,
ensamble molecular;
autocuchara.

Cyborg nutrition,
molecular assembly;
autospoon.

XIII

Investigación:
cnidarios mercuriales
en Yatsushiro.

Investigation:
mercurial cnidarians
in Yatsushiro.
